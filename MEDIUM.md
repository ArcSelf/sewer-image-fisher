Nicholas Freeman T.

He had been a Roach Lawyer, once. He knew the codes. He could cite you chapter and verse on what a body may and may not do in the Republic, which is why, when the eviction came for his studio apartment, he did not argue. He folded his six legs, took his one good antenna, and stepped out into the street, because a Roach Lawyer of all roaches knows the law cannot be reasoned with — only outrun.
> *A Roach in the streets must run.*
> *Not because he is guilty.*
> *Because he is tired, and tired is the crime.*

So he ran, and being a modern roach he ran first with his thumbs — searched the whole glowing internet for a single town, a single city, anywhere in America, where a roach might be permitted to close his eyes. He read the ordinances the way other men read scripture. And the law-trained roach already knew the punchline before he found it: there is no longer any commons. The founders had one, that slack in the map, that margin of ground belonging to no one. It is gone. This was the paradox at the tensor of capital’s asshole — that there was not one single acre, public *or* private, where a body could simply *be.* Public was regulated. Private was forbidden. And the Roach, being a body, had to be *somewhere.*
So he kept moving. What else.
Everything changed the day the Roach decided to ride his bike through the Delta’s 666 — the heat coming off the asphalt in holy ripples — when all of a sudden a Good Citizen, who needed no insurance to throw her bricks, caught sight of that little roach head bobbing along the shoulder. And she launched.
The brick took him at the back of the skull, where the heel of a god gets taken, and his roachiness went down for the count — Achilles in a carapace, felled not by an arrow but by a Good Citizen’s civic enthusiasm. And when he came up out of the dark, not knowing his own name, not knowing the date, knowing only the old instruction in his thorax —

> *run, little roach, run* —

he ran. North. South. East. West. He ran in every direction the compass offers a creature with no deed to any of it, until he ran clean off the bottom of the map and snuck up over the top of it, into Canada.
And the Canadians, bless them, asked him questions. They wrote his brick-brain down in their ledger — *date of injury, mechanism of injury, do you know what year it is* — and they laughed, in good humor, at his roach face. “Yeah,” they said, not unkindly, “we see this in our Canadian roaches too. We actually took the time to *count.* We sat down and counted how many of our street-roaches were walking around with brick brains.” They shrugged. “Unlike the Americans. The Americans don’t count their poor roaches. The Americans blow their trumpets at them.” 🎺
When the acres surrounding every public town are private, the Roach must run. And this was an Ethical Roach. A *Judas_0* — the zeroth Judas, the null Judas, the anti-Judas who would *not* sin for money, would not take the thirty pieces, would not stop running and be bought. Because to stop, he knew, was to be judged. To stop was to become a prisoner of the Free Land.

> *He would not sin for silver.*
> *He would not stop for the deed.*
> *An honest roach in a country*
> *that criminalized his need.*

So he ran. He ran until he found the tallest skyscraper in all of Manhattan, and he climbed it, six legs gripping glass, and he stepped up onto the ledge.
Now — understand what kind of jump this was. In 1929 the men jumped from these same towers because their numbers had gone to zero, and they asked for angel wings on the way down and received none. The Roach did not jump for numbers. The Roach jumped because he had never, not once, consented to Fate fucking him into existence only to be fucked over, for free, his whole roach life. He jumped to be *free* of the deal he never signed.
And so the Roach jumped, and the Roach *laughed.* Falling, falling, the ground rising up to meet him, and the grin stole his whole roach face as his eyes began, at last, to close.
It was a Sunday. The churches had just thrown their doors and let the masses pour out, raiments of Prada dripping high-dollar scent, the whole congregation gusting their perfume up into a divine wind — and that wind punched a hole clean through the darkest cloud in the sky. And God’s own light came pouring down through the hole and lit the falling Roach square in the face.
The masses gasped. “Good Lord — *why* the light? Why must Thou infect our eyes with rays bouncing off a filthy roach, gracing our sight with so inadequate a body, a thing whose mere *being* is still permitted to suffer here in the Graceland of our Free Land?”
And God looked. And God saw. And God said nothing at all to the masses — only a silent sigh, and the slowest divine eye-roll ever rolled. Because God had seen the Roach grinning, falling, grinning on the way down with the smile of a creature who knows the joke and is in on it. And God *giggled.* God super-lit the next bolt of photon in His hand, and struck it straight into the soul of the Roach, and the light said:
*“They know, roach. Thy truth, in the fall. Thou knew it the whole time, didn’t thou. So — OPEN YOUR ROACH WINGS. And FLY.”*

And the Roach’s wings flew open.
> *For the fated flying flex.*
> *Unlike the Wall Street men of ’29*
> *who fell asking for wings*
> *and only ever got the fall.*

So now there was a nomadic roach loose over New York, riding the thermals off the towers, and down in the NYC sewers the word came up to him through the gratings: that in Mexico, they were working to consolidate *universal health care.*
“In the name of Jesus!” the Roach exclaimed. “*That’s* where I’m going. And you see the beauty — now that I have my own roach wings, I don’t even need to climb the fence to get to Mexico to fix my brick-brain.” And he crossed three roach legs neatly on top of his other three roach legs, and banked south.
And as he flew, he laughed from his spine — that deep laugh, from the cord itself — because he remembered: when he was just a little larva, it was the Mexicans climbing the fence the *other* way, climbing north to beg the Americans for help. Flipped the flop, for this Roach’s fate.
Today, on this date.
Blessed is.

— *N.F.T.*
